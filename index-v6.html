The next step is not “more agents.”

The next step is a clean implementation sprint: harden the foundation, organize the company source of truth, improve the current agents, and give Tylor and Abel seamless access to Matrix-level context around the priorities that surface in their 1:1s.

90 days to reduce ambiguity before it becomes operational drag.

1. Safer AI Foundation

Credential review, access control, permissions, read-only-first rules, and human approval for sensitive actions.

2. Company Source of Truth

Turn scattered company context, decisions, workflows, and open loops into a usable internal intelligence layer.

3. Seamless Leader Workflows

Oscar first, then flexible Matrix-enabled work with Tylor and Abel based on what actually matters in their 1:1s.

Proposed 90-Day Sprint

Read-only first · Approval-gated
Week 1

Security + Access Control

Make the foundation safe before expanding data access or automation.

  • Credential and agent access audit
  • Permission map
  • Safe integration checklist
Weeks 2–4

Oscar Source of Truth

Turn Oscar’s company context into a usable map for the rest of the system.

  • Company/entity map
  • Decision log format
  • Executive brief rhythm
Weeks 5–10

Tylor + Abel: Matrix-Level Intelligence

Do not pre-decide a rigid worklist. Use 1:1s to identify priorities, then give each leader relevant Matrix-level context in a clean, professional way.

  • Working-session summaries and priority map
  • Role-specific Matrix access plan
  • Briefs, workflow maps, specs, and next-action views
Weeks 11–13

Consolidation + Readiness

Bring workstreams together and turn what was learned into the next Matrix build sequence.

  • Source-of-truth v2
  • Leadership rhythm
  • Next 90-day roadmap

Weeks 5–10: What changes

  • Focused 1:1 working sessions with Tylor and Abel.
  • Relevant company source-of-truth, decision history, workflows, and open loops made accessible without overexposing sensitive data.
  • Finance, customer, operations, support, systems, or buildout priorities mapped only after the actual need is clear.
  • Automation candidates prioritized only after owner, workflow, data source, risk level, and approval path are defined.

Scope control

This is not unlimited development and not a full deployment of every future Matrix capability.

  • No financial write-actions without approval.
  • No customer-facing automation without review.
  • No production system changes without explicit permission.
  • No vague “AI help” bucket without owner, workflow, and business outcome.
